Discover a strategy that looks beyond the benchmark

The Reams Unconstrained Fixed Income Strategy seeks to maximize risk-adjusted total return by systematically pursuing relative value opportunities throughout all sectors of the fixed income market. Our disciplined investment process attempts to identify and select the most attractive sectors, industries and individual securities available at any given point in time.

  • Decoupled from benchmarks
    Bond managers are often incentivized to “hug the benchmark,” which can lead to sub-optimal allocation decisions. Decoupling from benchmarks eliminates the pressure to invest in potentially unattractive sectors and provides the freedom to invest in different areas of the market, at different concentrations, in an effort to maximize total return.
  • Access to “best ideas”
    Unconstrained investing allows managers to take meaningful active positions wherever attractive risk-adjusted returns may be available across various geographies, sectors and individual credits. Portfolios are not overly-diversified, diluting alpha potential, and will only hold positions intended to drive total returns.
  • Tactical asset allocation
    Opportunities often arise quickly and much of the upside potential associated with unconstrained investing can come from tactical allocation decisions, rather than bottom-up decisions. We believe managers with a broad mandate and asset allocation discretion are often in the best position to capture these opportunities in an efficient and timely manner.
  • Flexible strategy
    Our approach seeks to outperform the broad fixed income market in a variety of interest rate and credit cycles. We utilize multiple alpha sources and are agnostic about return drivers in any given period, adapting to the unique dynamics of the current environment.

What Separates Reams from the Crowd

  • Differentiated View of Risk
    Reams defines risk as permanent loss of principal or the inability to meet investment objectives, not volatility or tracking error. We believe that adherence to backward-looking risk measures can negatively influence decision-making and erode long-term returns.
  • Focus on Total Return
    Many bond managers build portfolios for yield alone and maintain active risk positions at all times. Reams takes a full-cycle, total return viewpoint, evaluating each sector and individual security on the basis of its upside versus downside potential.
  • Reactive, not Predictive
    Reams reacts to relative value opportunities as they arise while also maintaining valuation discipline, rather than relying on economic forecasting and attempting to predict market movements.
  • Embracing Volatility
    Reams believes that the fixed income market is inherently volatile from both an interest rate and credit perspective. As a result we strive to construct portfolios that will not only withstand periods of heightened volatility, but also benefit from them.
